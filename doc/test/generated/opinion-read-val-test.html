I can eat fish for the first time in life! What should I make/eat/buy?

Cooking

Author user2

Published 1 day, 13 hours ago

Updated 1 day, 13 hours ago

I’ve suffered from a severe allergy to fish since childhood. If I ate any type of fish (excluding shellfish, I’ve been eating that my whole life) I’d go into anaphylactic shock. I was hospitalized several times for it as a kid and carried around an epipen for most of my adulthood preparing for the worst. Well, somehow, after 30+ years, I’ve grown out of it. I’ve been skin tested, blood tested, and as of last night have completed an eating test. I can now eat fish, any type. And I’m all in.

My question to you all - what fish dishes should I eat? I’m pumped to try a fish filet from McDs, some sushi, battered catfish, fish sticks, and all the other things I’ve heard of and had to avoid like the plague. I’m making a list and will be checking things off one by one.

No recipe is too daunting for me, no dish too wild, no restaurant too cheap - I’m here for all the flavors. Thank you in advance for helping me explore this tasty new chapter of my life!
